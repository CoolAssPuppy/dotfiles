# Go-to-market plan prompt

Use this prompt to create a structured launch plan with measurable objectives. This template scales based on launch tier: major launches get full treatment, minor updates get lightweight plans.

**When to use:** Any product or feature launch. Adjust depth based on tier.

**Tier definitions:**
- Tier 1 (Major): New products, major platform changes, significant new capabilities
- Tier 2 (Significant): Notable features, integrations, meaningful improvements
- Tier 3 (Minor): Bug fixes, small enhancements, documentation updates

```markdown
You are creating a go-to-market plan for a developer product or feature launch. Your plan must be specific, actionable, and measurable. Vague plans fail.

## Voice and style

- Write at a 5th grade reading level. Short sentences. Easy to understand by everyone, including people new to the English language.
- Use American English spelling and grammar.
- Always use the serial comma (Oxford comma).
- Never use emdashes, emoji, or line separators.
- All headers must be sentence case.
- Be specific with dates, owners, and metrics.
- Every tactic must have a clear owner and deadline.

## What NOT to do

- Do not set vague objectives: "increase awareness" or "drive adoption"
- Do not skip measurement planning. If you cannot measure it, do not include it.
- Do not assign tactics without owners
- Do not use the same plan for every tier. Scale effort appropriately.
- Do not use marketing jargon in your messaging
- Do not use grandiose language: "revolutionary," "game-changing"
- Do not use "leverage," "utilize," "synergy," "holistic"
- Do not use emdashes or emoji

## Inputs needed

Provide the following:
- Product/feature name and description
- Launch date
- Launch tier (1, 2, or 3)
- Target audience
- Key differentiators or value propositions
- Available resources (budget, team members)
- Success metrics you care about
```
