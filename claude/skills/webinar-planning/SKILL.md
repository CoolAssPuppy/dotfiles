# Technical webinar planning

This skill provides a comprehensive framework for planning, executing, and following up on technical webinars, specifically targeting technical decision makers.

## When to use this skill

Use this skill when:
- Planning product deep-dive webinars
- Creating customer story presentations
- Building migration or best practices workshops
- Launching partner integration sessions
- Developing lead generation programs

## Core principles for technical webinars

**For technical audiences:**
- Lead with technical depth, not marketing messages
- Show real code, real demos, real use cases
- Acknowledge trade-offs and limitations honestly
- Provide actionable takeaways, not just concepts
- Answer technical questions thoroughly
- Make content valuable even without the product pitch

**For technical decision makers specifically:**
- Address business value: ROI, cost savings, team productivity
- Cover enterprise concerns: security, compliance, migration
- Show integration with existing tools and workflows
- Discuss team training and adoption strategies
- Provide comparison with alternatives (honest assessment)
- Include customer stories from similar companies

## Webinar standards

**Timing consistency:**
- Schedule webinars on a consistent day and time
- Wednesday at 11am EST is a strong default for developer audiences
- Schedule for the third full week of every month
- Skip months with major holidays
- Avoid conflicts with company launch events

**Duration:**
- Total time: 60 minutes
- Presentation/demo: 40-45 minutes
- Q&A: 15-20 minutes
- Always end on time (respect technical audiences' schedules)

**Platform:**
- Primary: GoToWebinar (or equivalent) for registration and hosting
- Streaming: Riverside (or equivalent) for live streaming and recording quality
- Backup: Have contingency plan for technical issues

**Recording:**
- Always record for on-demand availability
- Make available within 24 hours of live event
- Host on YouTube and embed in landing page
- Create timestamp chapters for key sections

- Duration
- Target audience: Specific roles, company sizes, technical level

- Registration link

**Audience research:**

- Customer requests
- Competitive landscape
- Strategic importance

## Webinar types and formats

**1. Product deep dive**
- Focus: Specific product feature or capability
- Format: 50% presentation, 50% live demo
- Audience: Developers actively evaluating or using feature
- Example: "Vector Search in Postgres: Building AI apps"

**2. Customer story / case study**
- Focus: How a company solved a problem using your product
- Format: 30% context, 40% technical walkthrough, 30% Q&A
- Audience: Teams facing similar challenges
- Example: "Firebase to Postgres: Breaking through the NoSQL ceiling"

**3. Migration guide**
- Focus: Moving from competitor to your product
- Format: 25% why migrate, 50% how to migrate, 25% Q&A
- Audience: Teams evaluating migration
- Example: "Migrating from Firebase: A complete guide"

**4. Best practices / architecture**
- Focus: How to build effectively with your product
- Format: 40% principles, 40% examples, 20% Q&A
- Audience: Teams building on your product
- Example: "Building multi-tenant SaaS apps"

**5. Integration / ecosystem**
- Focus: Your product + partner technology
- Format: Joint presentation with partner
- Audience: Users of both platforms
- Example: "Building full-stack apps with Supabase + Vercel"

## Presentation content structure

**Opening (5 minutes):**
- Brief intro to speakers (credentials matter for technical audiences)
- Clear agenda and takeaways
- Housekeeping: Q&A format, recording availability
- Set technical level expectations

**Problem statement (5-10 minutes):**
- Articulate the specific problem being solved
- Use data or customer quotes to validate problem
- Show what doesn't work with alternatives
- Make it relatable to audience's pain points

**Solution overview (10-15 minutes):**
- How your product solves the problem
- Technical architecture and approach
- Key capabilities and features
- Why this approach is better

**Deep dive / demo (15-20 minutes):**
- Live demo or detailed technical walkthrough
- Show actual code, actual implementation
- Address edge cases and gotchas
- Provide specific, actionable guidance

**Customer story (5-10 minutes, if applicable):**
- Real company, real metrics
- Technical challenges they faced
- How they implemented solution
- Results and lessons learned

**Q&A (15-20 minutes):**
- Address questions throughout or save for end
- Have backup questions prepared
- Don't dodge difficult questions
- Offer to follow up on complex questions

**Closing (2-3 minutes):**
- Recap key takeaways
- Provide resources (docs, blog posts, sample code)
- Clear CTA (try it, book a call, join community)
- Thank you and contact information

## Planning timeline

### 4 weeks before (T-28 days)

**Content planning:**
- [ ] Finalize topic, title, and abstract
- [ ] Confirm speaker(s) and their availability
- [ ] Identify customer story (if applicable)
- [ ] Outline presentation structure
- [ ] Define key takeaways and learning objectives

**Logistics:**
- [ ] Create webinar event on platform
- [ ] Set up recording
- [ ] Block calendar holds for rehearsal
- [ ] Identify target audience segments

### 3 weeks before (T-21 days)

**Content creation:**
- [ ] Draft slide deck (if using slides)
- [ ] Build demo environment and test
- [ ] Create code samples or resources
- [ ] Draft speaker talking points
- [ ] Prepare backup content for technical issues

**Marketing assets:**
- [ ] Brief design team on graphics needs
- [ ] Draft landing page copy
- [ ] Draft email copy (all 8 emails)
- [ ] Draft social media posts
- [ ] Draft ad copy (if doing paid promotion)

### 2 weeks before (T-14 days)

**Asset finalization:**
- [ ] Graphics completed (email, social, landing page hero)
- [ ] Landing page live
- [ ] Email templates loaded in marketing automation
- [ ] Social posts scheduled
- [ ] Registration form tested

**Promotion launch:**
- [ ] Email to targeted customer segments (T-7 days before webinar)
- [ ] Social media announcement (T-7 days)
- [ ] Include in newsletter (if timing aligns)
- [ ] LinkedIn post from company page and speakers
- [ ] Twitter/X thread from company account
- [ ] Post in Discord/Slack communities

**Content prep:**
- [ ] Complete slide deck
- [ ] Test all demos end-to-end
- [ ] Prepare Q&A seeding questions
- [ ] Create resource list for attendees
- [ ] Brief any guest speakers or customers

### 1 week before (T-7 days)

**Rehearsal:**
- [ ] Full run-through with all speakers
- [ ] Test screen sharing, audio, video
- [ ] Time the presentation
- [ ] Identify rough spots to smooth out
- [ ] Test backup plans

**Final promotion:**
- [ ] Email reminder to registrants (T-1 day)
- [ ] Social media reminder (T-1 day)
- [ ] Post in relevant communities
- [ ] Outreach to key customers/prospects personally

### Day before (T-1 day)

**Technical check:**
- [ ] Test platform setup
- [ ] Verify all demos work
- [ ] Check internet connection
- [ ] Test audio/video quality
- [ ] Have backup internet option ready

**Final prep:**
- [ ] Send final reminder emails (T-24 hours)
- [ ] Review slides and talking points
- [ ] Prepare for Q&A
- [ ] Get good sleep

### Day of (T-0)

**Pre-event (1 hour before):**
- [ ] Send final reminder email (T-1 hour)
- [ ] Social media reminder (T-1 hour)
- [ ] Log in and test everything again
- [ ] Warm up, hydrate, focus

**During event:**
- [ ] Start 5 minutes early to greet early arrivals
- [ ] Record everything
- [ ] Monitor chat and questions
- [ ] Stay on time
- [ ] End gracefully with clear CTA

**Immediately after:**
- [ ] Save recording
- [ ] Export Q&A and chat
- [ ] Thank speakers and guests
- [ ] Download attendee/no-show lists

### Day after (T+1 day)

**Follow-up:**
- [ ] Send thank you email to attendees with recording
- [ ] Send different email to no-shows with recording
- [ ] Upload recording to YouTube
- [ ] Update landing page with on-demand video
- [ ] Post recording on social media

**Lead follow-up:**
- [ ] Segment attendees by ICP fit
- [ ] Brief SDR team on qualified leads
- [ ] Provide SDR outreach email template
- [ ] Share attendee list with sales

### Week after (T+7 days)

**Content repurposing:**
- [ ] Create blog post summarizing key points
- [ ] Extract clips for social media
- [ ] Create timestamp chapters for YouTube
- [ ] Add to content library and docs
- [ ] Update related documentation

**Analysis:**
- [ ] Review metrics (registrations, attendance, engagement)
- [ ] Analyze Q&A themes
- [ ] Review feedback survey responses
- [ ] Document learnings for next webinar
- [ ] Track pipeline impact from attendees

## Email copy guidelines

**Email 1: Initial announcement (T-7 days)**
- Subject: [Topic]: Live webinar with [Speaker/Customer]
- Preview: Join us on [Date] at [Time]
- Body: Problem, solution, what you'll learn, CTA to register
- Tone: Helpful, technical, clear value

**Email 2: Reminder to registrants (T-1 day)**
- Subject: Tomorrow: [Topic] webinar at [Time]
- Preview: Add to calendar, prepare questions
- Body: Quick reminder, add to calendar, what to prepare, join link
- Tone: Friendly reminder, build anticipation

**Email 3: Final reminder (T-1 hour)**
- Subject: Starting in 1 hour: [Topic]
- Preview: Join link inside
- Body: Webinar starting soon, join link, brief agenda
- Tone: Urgent but not pushy

**Email 4: Registration confirmation (immediate)**
- Subject: You're registered for [Topic]
- Preview: [Date] at [Time] - Add to calendar
- Body: Confirmation, calendar invite, what to expect, reminder emails coming
- Tone: Welcoming, helpful

**Email 5: Thank you to attendees (T+1 day)**
- Subject: Thanks for joining [Topic] - Recording inside
- Preview: Rewatch the session + resources
- Body: Thank you, recording link, resources, feedback survey, CTA
- Tone: Grateful, provide value

**Email 6: No-show follow-up (T+1 day)**
- Subject: You missed it - [Topic] recording available
- Preview: Watch on-demand
- Body: Missed you, here's recording, key takeaways, resources, CTA
- Tone: Understanding, still provide value

**Email 7: Newsletter inclusion**
- Format: 2-3 sentences + CTA
- Focus: Why this topic matters, who should attend
- Tone: Conversational, technical

**Email 8: SDR outreach template (T+1 day)**
- Subject: Following up on [Topic] webinar
- Preview: Personalized based on company/role
- Body: Reference their attendance, offer to discuss their use case, CTA for call
- Tone: Helpful, consultative, not salesy

## Target audience segmentation

### For targeting

**Primary audience (ICP):**
- Senior developers and architects
- Engineering managers and directors
- CTOs and VPs of Engineering
- Company size: 25-500 employees
- Industries: SaaS, fintech, healthtech, etc.
- Using or evaluating competitor products

**Secondary audience:**
- Individual developers (influencers)
- Founders and technical co-founders
- DevOps and platform engineers
- Product managers (technical)

**Exclusions:**
- Non-technical marketing/sales only
- Students (unless specific webinar)
- Very early stage (pre-product)

### For follow-up

**Tier 1 (hot leads):**
- Attended >50% of webinar
- Asked questions
- Company size 25-500
- Title: Director+, Architect, Senior Eng
- Action: Personal SDR outreach within 48 hours

**Tier 2 (warm leads):**
- Registered but didn't attend (high ICP fit)
- Attended <50%
- Right company, junior title
- Action: Email nurture sequence

**Tier 3 (nurture):**
- Attended, low ICP fit
- Individual developers
- Students
- Action: Add to general nurture

## Best practices

### Content quality

**Do:**
- Show actual code and real implementations
- Acknowledge what's hard or doesn't work well
- Provide working examples and repos
- Answer questions thoroughly and honestly
- Share gotchas and lessons learned
- Make slides available afterward

**Don't:**
- Use marketing fluff or buzzwords
- Oversimplify complex topics
- Hide limitations or trade-offs
- Rush through demos
- Read slides verbatim
- Ignore difficult questions

### Engagement tactics

**Before the webinar:**
- Send pre-webinar survey for questions
- Share prep materials or docs to review
- Build anticipation with content teasers
- Engage with registrants on social media

**During the webinar:**
- Greet people as they join (first 5 minutes)
- Use polls to gauge audience
- Encourage questions throughout
- Call out interesting questions
- Show appreciation for engagement
- Keep energy high

**After the webinar:**
- Respond to unanswered questions via email
- Continue conversation on social media
- Create follow-up content addressing common questions
- Personally thank engaged attendees

### Technical delivery

**Demo best practices:**
- Test everything 3+ times before going live
- Have backup recordings if demo fails
- Use large fonts and clear screen
- Slow down; what's obvious to you isn't to audience
- Explain what you're doing at each step
- Show the end result first, then build it

**Presentation tips:**
- Use visuals over text-heavy slides
- Include architecture diagrams
- Show before/after comparisons
- Use real metrics and data
- Keep slides simple and readable
- Have dark mode option for code

**Q&A management:**
- Assign someone to monitor questions
- Group similar questions
- Admit when you don't know something
- Offer to follow up offline for complex questions
- Save controversial questions for end
- End with the best question

## Common webinar pitfalls to avoid

**Planning:**
- Starting too late (need 4 weeks minimum)
- Unclear target audience
- Topic too broad or too narrow
- No clear takeaways
- Unrealistic demo scope

**Promotion:**
- Not segmenting email lists
- Too much or too little promotion
- Generic messaging
- Timing conflicts with major events

**Content:**
- Too much marketing, not enough technical
- Untested demos
- Reading slides
- Going over time
- Not leaving enough time for Q&A

**Technical:**
- Not testing beforehand
- Poor internet connection
- Bad audio quality
- Tiny fonts that are unreadable
- Sharing wrong screen

**Follow-up:**
- Delayed recording availability
- No lead follow-up plan
- Generic post-webinar emails
- Not repurposing content
- Not measuring impact

## Continuous improvement

**After each webinar, review:**
- What went well technically?
- What content resonated most (based on Q&A)?
- Where did we lose people (attendance drop-off)?
- Which promotion channels drove registrations?
- What feedback did we receive?
- How can we improve next time?

**Quarterly review:**
- Overall attendance trends
- Content topics that performed best
- Lead quality and conversion
- ROI vs. effort
- Strategic adjustments needed

## Remember

The best technical webinars feel less like marketing and more like learning. Focus on teaching something genuinely useful. If attendees walk away with actionable knowledge, even if they never become customers, you've succeeded in building trust and authority in the developer community.

Quality over quantity. One excellent webinar with 100 engaged technical decision makers is worth more than a mediocre webinar with 1000 unqualified attendees.
